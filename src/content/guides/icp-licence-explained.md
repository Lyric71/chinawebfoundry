## What is an ICP licence?

An ICP (Internet Content Provider) licence is a permit issued by China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT). Without one, your website cannot legally be hosted on a server inside mainland China.

There are two main types:

- **ICP Bei'an (ICP filing)** - required for all websites hosted in China, including informational and marketing sites
- **ICP Zheng (ICP commercial licence)** - required if your website involves online transactions or paid services

## Why it matters

If you want your WordPress site to load fast for users in mainland China, you need hosting inside the country. And hosting inside the country requires an ICP licence. There is no shortcut.

Sites hosted outside China can still be accessed, but they face slow load times and potential blocking by the Great Firewall.

## How to apply

The process typically takes 2-4 weeks and involves:

- A Chinese business entity or local sponsor
- Server details from your hosting provider
- Identity verification of the site administrator
- Submission through your hosting provider's portal

## Common pitfalls

- Applying without a Chinese business entity or local partner
- Mismatch between domain registration details and licence application
- Hosting provider not registered with MIIT
- Incomplete documentation causing repeated rejections
